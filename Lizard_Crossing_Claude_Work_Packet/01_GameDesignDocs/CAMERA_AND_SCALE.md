# CAMERA AND SCALE

## Main camera rule
The camera must sell the tiny lizard fantasy before any other system matters.

## Camera placement
- Low third-person camera behind the lizard.
- Lizard visible near bottom center.
- Camera close to sidewalk height.
- Slightly above the lizard, looking forward.
- Wide enough FOV to see sideways crossing hazards.
- Smooth follow with slight lag.
- Subtle camera shake for impacts.
- Tiny bounce or vibration while moving.

## Scale targets
Lizard:
- small but readable
- about 1 unit long in prototype scale

Human shoes:
- huge compared to lizard
- should feel like 8x-15x bigger than lizard length
- soles should fill large parts of the screen when close

Cars/scooters:
- tires and wheels should be huge moving walls
- scale must create fear/readability

Sidewalk:
- cracks should feel like trenches
- debris should feel meaningful

## Camera failure signs
The camera is wrong if:
- it feels like a normal third-person game
- the lizard feels human-sized
- the sidewalk looks flat and empty
- shoes do not feel threatening
- hazards move forward with the lizard instead of sideways across the path
